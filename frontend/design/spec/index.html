00
// doctrine

Six laws. Everything else is taste.

law 01 · proved by: spacex · okx · robinhood · wealthsimple · ai4finance
Real numbers, dated.

No invented stats, no round marketing numbers, no counters on filler. Every figure carries its provenance: PF 8.72 · as of 2026-06-29. The timestamp is the argument.

law 02 · proved by: every reference in the mine, without exception
Color is instrumentation.

Emerald means interactive or alive. Green means profit, copper means loss — money only. Everything else is monochrome. A colored pixel is a reading on a gauge, never paint.

law 03 · proved by: rogo · hebbia · robinhood · magma · our live v7
Two voices, one page.

Fraunces at weight 400 — never bolded, scale does the shouting — for the human claim. Geist Mono for the machine's testimony. Geist Sans is connective tissue and stays out of the way.

law 04 · proved by: x.ai · graphite luminance steps · okx · cursor
Hairline flatness.

Depth = one surface step + a 1px hairline. No glass, no glow, no drop shadows on content. Two sanctioned exceptions: the footer bloom, and one luminous-type hero moment per site, if earned.

law 05 · proved by: nextjs one-entrance · graphite one-pin · cursor diegetic
One motion moment.

One entrance per view, then stillness. One scroll-pinned sequence per page, maximum. All other motion is diegetic — inside terminals and charts, where the product is visibly working. Everything rides one curve.

law 06 · proved by: ai4finance's failure (blank page without JS)
Content-first, always.

Every page renders complete without JavaScript. Scripts enhance — they never gate. Reduced-motion collapses every animation to its final state. Skip links, SR descriptions, dialog semantics ship by default.

Are there usage limits?

No artificial caps on the self-hosted stack. Throughput is bounded only by your infrastructure and your provider's limits.

Do I bring my own model keys?

Yes — any LiteLLM-supported provider or a local model. Keys stay on your infrastructure.
